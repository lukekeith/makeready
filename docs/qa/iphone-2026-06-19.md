# Release Notes — 2026-06-19

> Window: since the 2026-06-12 1:56 AM archive (v1.1.3, build 131) — 19 commits. (Sized for TestFlight's 4,000-char field.)

Membership management is the headline of this build. Group leaders can now act on join requests directly: tapping a pending request opens a centered modal showing who asked, which group, and the exact date and time they requested — with Approve, Reject, and Cancel. Approving or rejecting updates instantly, clearing the request and its red-dot indicator on the group card before the list reconciles with the server. A new "Change membership" flow lets you remove a member from a group, rejoin one who was removed, or transfer a member into another group they're not yet in — picked from a swipe-driven group selector with a confirmation step, all animated as one connected panel you can swipe back down to dismiss.

The Members tab gained a Members / Non-members filter. "Non-members" surfaces people who used to be associated with the org through a group but no longer are — each card explains *why* (Removed from membership, Removed from organization, or Request rejected) and *when*, backed by a new server-side membership audit trail. A non-member card no longer shows a group badge: that group association is reserved for people who are actually current members of a group, so a former member's old group name no longer reads as a live association.

Bible search got noticeably smarter on the backend the app talks to. Searches now fuse keyword and meaning-based matching, run results through a cross-encoder reranker, and expand queries with generated paraphrases (doc2query) so concept and topic searches surface the right passages more reliably. The in-app search field reflects this — the placeholder now reads "Search reference or topic" instead of only suggesting a verse reference.

Sign-in was simplified under the hood: the app now resolves its environment automatically on login — using the local server when it's reachable and falling back to production otherwise — instead of branching on a hardcoded dev check. For most testers this is invisible; it matters only on debug builds switching between Local and Production.

Not in the iPhone app (web/server only, listed so you don't go looking): the SMS join flow made phone-number consent optional for Twilio A2P compliance, the member web experience gained a study progress bar and native scroll across reading themes and dropped its legacy bottom nav, plus CI/deploy plumbing for Bible-search backfill and image promotion.

Please focus testing on: the join-request response modal (approve and reject a pending request, confirm the red dot and count clear on the group card and group-home), the Change membership flow (remove a member, rejoin them, and transfer one into another group — including swiping the group selector back down to cancel), the Members tab Members/Non-members filter (confirm non-member cards show the correct reason and date and show no group badge), and Bible search (try concept/topic queries like "forgiveness" or "David and Goliath" and tap through to the reader).
